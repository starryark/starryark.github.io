## Me:

I graduated with a B.S. in Molecular and Cell Biology from UC San Diego, Class of '26. After contemplating my life, education, future, and so forth, I decided I do not wish to enter or follow the rat race ahead. 

Instead, I wish to explore something truly innovative that adds value in perhaps instrumental but definitely in a Kantian sense.

### Artificial Intelligence

I also like AI. I believe technology will transform humanity, starting with the younger generation. I'm speaking from my peronal experience seeing technology impacting my schooling. 

After hearing countless talks from old and powerful profs roasting AI (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, etc.) hallucinations—who have no clue how LLMs work since they are too occupied with the vulgarities of their life and their pitiful paychecks—I found pockets of seminar discussions that I believe are truly productive from people in machine learning, neuroscience, and other mingled disciplines. 

Thus, I want to pivot away from the groups of people that I think are counterproductive and pivot toward what I think is truly productive for the future.

Sometimes, when I look at the fallacies and pointlessness of the older generation(s), I lose hope for the future, especially when seeing that my peers who are most like them are often the most successful. 

But looking at the younger generation always gives me new hope, especially with the new, changing technology that they (perhaps myself?) will have most of their lifetimes to utilize.

Now with almost all knowlege floating arround on the internet, and LLMs that greatly reduced the information assymetry of almost anyone. I believe the future will become more productive if we were to throw certain things away, forget certain bad human habits and tear certain institutions down.
